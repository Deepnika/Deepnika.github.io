About Me

I'm Deepnika Jain, a senior year undergraduate student at National Institute of Technology Karnataka. My B.Tech major is Materials Science, although I have been aiming to study astronomy in graduate school. I am fascinated by the inherent complexities in the small and large scale physics and their intersection. My goal is to understand physics of gravitational-waves and use them to solve astronomical problems.







Experience

During my undergraduate study, I was a Visiting Undergraduate Research student at California Institute of Technology. There I studied Superluminous Supernovae, which are core-collapse supernovae with progenitors approximately 10-100 times the mass of a normal core-collapse progenitor star. I also spent one night at the Palomar Observatory for observing some ZTF transients.
